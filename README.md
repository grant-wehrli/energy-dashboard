## 1. Topic Selection

**Theme:** Smart Home  

**Focus:** Designing interactive systems to help users interpret complex energy consumption data within a modern smart home environment.

## 2. Target Population

Our primary target population consists of environmentally conscious homeowners and tech-friendly residents, ages 25–60, who use smart home devices but feel overwhelmed by technical energy data.

This includes users who want to reduce their carbon footprint and lower their monthly utility bills. These users are likely digitally literate and cost-conscious, and they are likely to engage with a web-based tool. However, they are not experts in energy management or energy conservation.

## 3. Target Context

The problem occurs in the daily management of household utilities.

More specifically, users may check their energy provider’s app or smart home hub and see complex graphs or kilowatt-hour (kWh) metrics that are difficult to interpret. These systems often show energy usage but fail to clearly explain what caused the increase or what actions users can take to reduce consumption.

## 4. Problem Statement

Users can see how much energy they use, but they do not understand why their usage is high or how to reduce it without sacrificing comfort.

Without clear explanations and actionable recommendations, users may remain passive. This can lead to wasted energy, higher utility costs, and missed opportunities to develop more sustainable habits.

## 5. Limitations of Existing Systems

Existing systems often display energy data through complex or hard-to-understand graphs. Many users may not know how to interpret these graphs, understand technical jargon, or identify the next steps they should take to reduce their energy consumption efficiently.

As a result, these tools provide information but do not always support meaningful decision-making.

## 6. Proposed Solution Direction

We propose a web-based energy dashboard with two interface conditions for A/B comparison:

### Version A: Standard Dashboard

A standard chart-based dashboard showing appliance-level energy usage over time.

### Version B: AI-Augmented Dashboard

The same dashboard, enhanced with an AI natural language panel that explains usage patterns and integrates real weather forecast data to generate proactive warnings.

For example:

> A heatwave is forecast this week. Expect your AC costs to rise by 20–30% unless usage is adjusted.

## Core Research Question

Does an AI-generated natural language explanation improve user comprehension of energy data and increase stated intent to change behavior, compared to charts alone?
